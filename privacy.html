1. Information We Collect

When you interact with bXo Tutoring, we may collect the following personal information:

  • Full name, email address, and phone number so we can confirm bookings, respond to enquiries and contact you regarding lessons.
  • Information you provide when communicating with us (e.g., lesson goals, subject areas, and availability).
  • Payment details processed securely by trusted third-party providers (we do not store card details).
  • Non-identifying information such as website usage data (via cookies or analytics tools) to improve our services.

2. How We Use Information

We use your personal information to:

  • Deliver tutoring sessions online or in person.
  • Confirm, manage, and reschedule lesson bookings.
  • Process payments securely through third-party providers.
  • Respond to your enquiries and provide customer support.
  • Share lesson-related resources and updates.
  • Improve our services, website, and user experience.
  • Comply with legal obligations under Australian law.

We will not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties.

3. Data Security

We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data from unauthorised access, misuse, loss, or disclosure. These include:

  • Secure storage of digital records.
  • Access limited only to authorised staff.
  • Use of trusted third-party payment processors for transactions.

While we take all reasonable steps to secure your information, no system is completely immune from security risks.

4. Sharing of Information

We may share your personal information only when:

  • Required by law or regulatory authorities.
  • Necessary to complete a booking or payment via secure third-party providers.
  • With your explicit consent (e.g., if you request a referral or resource from an external provider).

5. Your Rights

Under the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth), you have the right to:

  • Request access to the personal data we hold about you.
  • Request correction of inaccurate or outdated information.
  • Request deletion of your data, subject to record-keeping obligations.
  • Make a complaint if you believe your privacy rights have been breached.

If you wish to exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details below. If you are not satisfied with our response, you may contact the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) for further assistance.

6. Retention of Data

We keep your personal data only for as long as it is needed for the purposes outlined in this policy or as required by Australian law.
