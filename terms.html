1. Services

bXo Tutoring provides biology tutoring services to students in New South Wales, Australia. Services may be delivered online or in person, depending on availability and prior agreement.

2. Bookings & Confirmation

When you request a tutoring session through our website:

  • You must provide accurate and complete personal information (name, email, phone number, subject requirements, and availability).
  • After selecting a time slot, you will receive an email or phone call to confirm your booking.
  • Your booking is not final until you have received confirmation from bXo Tutoring.

3. Payments

Once your booking is confirmed:

  • You will be sent instructions to complete payment through a secure third-party payment processor.
  • bXo Tutoring does not store or process your payment details directly.
  • Payment must be received prior to the tutoring session unless otherwise agreed in writing.

4. Cancellations & Rescheduling

  • If you need to cancel or reschedule, please notify us at least 24 hours in advance.
  • Cancellations made with less than 24 hours’ notice may incur a cancellation fee equal to the session cost.
  • If bXo Tutoring needs to cancel a session, you will be offered an alternative time or a full refund for that session.

5. Student Responsibilities

  • Be prepared and on time for each session.
  • Ensure you have access to the required technology for online sessions (e.g., a stable internet connection, webcam, microphone, and relevant software).
  • Respectful behaviour towards tutors is expected at all times.

6. Limitation of Liability

While bXo Tutoring aims to provide high-quality tutoring, we cannot guarantee specific academic outcomes. To the extent permitted by law:

  • We are not liable for any indirect, incidental, or consequential loss arising from the use of our services.
  • Our total liability for any claim related to our services is limited to the amount paid for the relevant tutoring session.

7. Governing Law

These Terms and Conditions are governed by the laws of New South Wales, Australia. Any disputes will be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of NSW.
